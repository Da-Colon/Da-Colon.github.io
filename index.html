Knife Safety

Speaking of knives one thing that I would train any new chef is to learn proper handling and storage of knives. In a professional kitchen these are perhaps the most utilized tool. If your knives are not maintained and properly cleaned and stored, not only can they cross-contaminate your diffrent ingrediants but a dull knife is more dangerous in some instances than a sharp one. For example if you are cutting something with soft skin such as a tomato and the knife is dull it can roll causing you to cut yourself pretty badly. I recommend using a cutting glove for those not use to using new knives. When holding something while cutting it's always a must to hold the item like a claw with your fingers tilted inside so if a roll does occur none of your fingers are in the direct path of a knife. They should always be cleaned and dryed after use and properly stored in the knife block or put away in a proper storage area. I cannot stress enough they anyone handling knives use caution and be aware of your surroundings.
